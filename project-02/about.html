About Me

A career that is built on service, problem-solving, and continued growth.

My professional journey has taken me through military service, skilled trades, construction leadership, inspections, plan review, and now technology.

My Professional Background

I am an Army veteran, licensed Master Plumber, and building-code professional with experience in construction, plumbing, inspections, code compliance, and plan review.

My work requires attention to detail, strong communication, practical problem-solving, and the ability to interpret technical documents. I work to help ensure that buildings and systems are safe, functional, and compliant with adopted codes.

My Education

I am completing a Bachelor of Science in Computing Applications at Texas Tech University. Through this program, I have developed experience with web development, project management, information organization, GitHub, and other digital tools.

My coursework has helped me connect my professional experience with technology while strengthening my ability to organize information, build digital projects, and communicate ideas clearly.

My Future Direction

I am preparing for a future career in cybersecurity, digital investigations, and technology-focused public service.

I am especially interested in work that combines technical knowledge with investigation, accountability, and public safety. I plan to continue building my skills through certifications, hands-on learning, and advanced education.

How I Approach My Work

I believe that experience from one career can create value in another. Construction taught me how systems fit together. Inspection taught me how to identify problems and verify compliance. Military service taught me responsibility, teamwork, and perseverance.

I bring those lessons into every new challenge. Whether I am reviewing a building plan, developing a website, or learning a cybersecurity concept, my goal is to understand the system, identify the details that matter, and complete the work correctly.
